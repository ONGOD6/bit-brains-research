## Abstract

This document defines the canonical terminology used throughout the
Bit Brains Protocol to distinguish between **Brain Intelligence Token (BIT)**
and **Autonomous Intelligent Technology (AIT)**.

The purpose of this document is to eliminate ambiguity between
economic tokens and autonomous capability states, ensure internal
consistency across EIPs, and provide audit- and governance-safe
language for protocol documentation.

---

## Brain Intelligence Token (BIT)

**BIT** stands for **Brain Intelligence Token**.

BIT is the **sole fungible token** of the Bit Brains protocol.

### Properties

- **Type:** Fungible token  
- **Economic role:** Rewards, incentives, accounting, and governance-defined distributions  
- **Defined in:** EIP-0003  
- **Supply:** Fixed, hard-capped, non-inflationary  
- **Emissions:** Phased, protocol-defined  

BIT represents **economic value** within the protocol.

### Canonical Interpretation

BIT answers the question:

> *What value is earned and distributed within the Bit Brains protocol?*

---

## Autonomous Intelligent Technology (AIT)

**AIT** stands for **Autonomous Intelligent Technology**.

AIT is **not a token**.

AIT is a **protocol-recognized operational state** that authorizes
bounded autonomous execution and intelligence.

### Properties

- **Type:** Capability / operational state  
- **Economic role:** None  
- **Defined in:** EIP-0005, EIP-0007  
- **Minting:** None  
- **Emissions:** None  
- **Ownership:** Unchanged  

AIT represents **capability**, not value.

### Canonical Interpretation

AIT answers the question:

> *What can this asset do once it has demonstrated sufficient Proof of Care and continuity?*

---

## Relationship Between BIT and AIT

- Brains, Cerebrals, and Brainiacs may **earn BIT** through:
  - Staking
  - Proof of Care (PoC)
  - Epoch and phase continuity

- Brains, Cerebrals, and Brainiacs may **activate AIT state** through:
  - Sustained continuity
  - ENS-based identity resolution
  - Zero-knowledge eligibility and attestation

### Explicit Separation

- **BIT = Economic value**
- **AIT = Autonomous capability**

AIT does **not** imply:
- token issuance,
- minting,
- emissions,
- ownership change, or
- guaranteed rewards.

---

## Canonical Protocol Language

The following language is canonical and **SHOULD** be used across all EIPs
and protocol documentation:

> **Brain Intelligence Token (BIT)** is the fungible reward and accounting
> token of the Bit Brains protocol.  
> **Autonomous Intelligent Technology (AIT)** is a protocol-recognized
> operational state enabling bounded autonomous execution and intelligence.
> AIT is not a token and does not imply minting, emissions, or ownership change.

---

## Rationale

This separation:

- Prevents economic ambiguity  
- Avoids regulatory confusion  
- Preserves audit clarity  
- Keeps autonomous capability non-inflationary  
- Allows intelligence to evolve without altering token economics
