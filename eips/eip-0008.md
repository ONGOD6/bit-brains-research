## Abstract

This EIP defines the Phase One Genesis reward distribution standard
for the Bit Brains protocol.

Phase One participation is restricted to **Genesis Paired Assets**,
composed of:
- a Genesis ERC-721 NFT (Brain or Pickle Punk), and
- its corresponding Ethscription artifact.

Phase One rewards are:
- treasury-funded,
- non-inflationary,
- settled once at Phase One close,
- routed through ENS, and
- verified using zero-knowledge (ZK) proofs.

All reward amounts are **TO BE DETERMINED**.

Eligibility is binary.
A qualifying Genesis Paired Asset receives rewards or zero.
Partial rewards are not permitted.

Any rewards not distributed due to ineligibility
return to the Rewards Reserve Treasury.

## Motivation

Phase One requires simple, auditable, and non-custodial reward rules
that favor long-term participation and continuity.

This EIP:
- establishes ENS as the canonical reward routing identity,
- requires continuous paired-asset staking,
- enforces Proof of Care continuity,
- uses ZK proofs for verification,
- prevents inflation under all outcomes, and
- defines forfeiture and treasury reversion rules.

## Scope

This EIP applies only to:
- Genesis Paired Assets derived from Genesis Brains and Pickle Punks, and
- Phase One Genesis rewards.

This EIP does not define:
- rewards for later phases,
- post-Genesis reward programs,
- node operation incentives,
- token issuance mechanics, or
- exchange or market behavior.

## Definitions

**Phase One**  
The first Genesis reward phase of the protocol.
Evaluation structure and duration are governance-defined.

**Genesis Paired Asset**  
The canonical Phase One participation unit composed of:
1. one Genesis ERC-721 NFT (Brain or Pickle Punk), and  
2. its corresponding Ethscription artifact.

Both components together form the sole qualifying unit for staking,
Proof of Care evaluation, and reward eligibility.

**Ethscription Artifact**  
An immutable on-chain calldata provenance record paired one-to-one
with a Genesis ERC-721 NFT.
Ethscriptions do not receive rewards independently but MUST remain
valid and in good standing for the Paired Asset to qualify.

---

## Eligibility Requirements (Normative)

To receive Phase One rewards, a Genesis Paired Asset MUST satisfy
**all** requirements for the entire Phase One evaluation window:

1. **Continuous Paired-Asset Staking**  
   Both the ERC-721 NFT and its paired Ethscription
   MUST remain valid and in protocol-defined good standing.
   Any unstaking, transfer, invalidation, or disassociation
   permanently disqualifies the Paired Asset.

2. **Proof of Care Continuity**  
   Proof of Care continuity MUST remain unbroken.
   Verified via protocol checks and ZK proofs.

3. **ENS-Bound Ownership**  
   A deterministic ENS subdomain under `bitbrains.eth`
   MUST resolve to the wallet staking the Paired Asset.
   Resolution is validated via ZK proofs at Phase One close.

Failure to meet any requirement results in permanent disqualification.

## Phase One Reward Authorization (Normative)

Phase One rewards, if authorized, MUST satisfy all constraints below:

- **Funding Source:** Rewards Reserve Treasury only  
- **Supply:** Non-inflationary  
- **Total Allocation:** CAPPED (TO BE DETERMINED)  
- **Per-Asset Allocation:** TO BE DETERMINED  
- **Settlement Timing:** Single settlement at Phase One close  

This EIP does not imply entitlement.
Rewards MAY be reduced, delayed, restructured, or set to zero.

---

## Settlement and Claims

Rewards are settled once, at Phase One close.

Claims are:
- user-initiated,
- gas-paid by the user,
- routed through ENS, and
- verified using zero-knowledge proofs.

The protocol MUST NOT automatically push rewards.

---

## Disqualification and Forfeiture

Genesis Paired Assets that fail eligibility requirements
receive zero rewards.

Rewards allocated to disqualified or non-participating
Paired Assets are not redistributed.

---

## Treasury Reversion

Any portion of the Phase One reward allocation
not distributed MUST be returned to the Rewards Reserve Treasury.

Unused rewards MUST NOT be routed to the Team Treasury.
Funds remain under protocol governance control.
