# EIP-0013-A — END DAO Governance & Controlled Maturation Framework

Status: Draft — Governance Architecture Consolidation  
Chain: Ethereum  
Token: BITS — Brain Intelligence Technology Services  
DAO Activation Date: March 1, 2027  

---

# 1. Governance Philosophy — Controlled Maturation

END DAO follows a **controlled maturation into decentralization** model.

The protocol is designed to:

- Protect early-stage integrity
- Prevent reckless governance capture
- Gradually diffuse control over time
- Preserve constitutional supply constraints
- Outlive its founders through structural dilution

Founders maintain early-stage influence and blocking capability to safeguard the protocol during formative years.  
This influence naturally dilutes through emissions, airdrops, and expanded participation.

This is intentional stewardship, not permanent control.

---

# 2. Constitutional Supply Structure

Total Supply Cap: 1,000,000,000 BITS (Fixed — Non-Inflationary)

Allocation:

- 20% — Founders & Team (Immutable)
- 10% — Continuity & Proof-of-Care Airdrop
- 20% — Market Allocation
- 50% — Node Reward Allocation (Emission-Based)

Supply cap cannot be increased by governance.  
Founder allocation cannot be expanded by governance.

---

# 3. Revenue Model

Revenue Streams:

- Brains-as-a-Service (BaaS)
- Software-as-a-Service (SaaS)
- Nodes-as-a-Service (NaaS)

Revenue Split:

Year 1 (March 1, 2026 – March 1, 2027):
- 50% Founders
- 50% Treasury Reserve

Post DAO Activation (March 1, 2027 onward):
- 70% Node Fund (DAO Treasury)
- 30% Founders (Immutable)

Revenue allocation is separate from token allocation.

---

# 4. Airdrop Model (10% Total)

Total Airdrop Pool: 100,000,000 BITS

Distribution:
- 2% of total supply per year
- 20,000,000 BITS annually
- 5-year duration

Eligibility weighted by:

- Artifact continuity integrity
- Proof of Care participation
- Governance-approved weighting within constitutional limits

Tokens receive voting rights upon distribution.

---

# 5. Presale Model (5% Total)

Total Presale Allocation: 50,000,000 BITS

Voting Rights:
- Fully voting-enabled immediately
- Vesting restricts transferability only

Vesting Schedule (30 Months):

- 1% at 12 months (10M)
- 1% at 18 months (10M)
- 1% at 24 months (10M)
- 2% at 30 months (20M)

---

# 6. Founder Voting Enablement

Total Founder Allocation: 200,000,000 BITS (20%)

At DAO Activation:
- 6% voting-enabled (60,000,000 BITS)

Additional voting unlock mirrors presale schedule:

- +1% at 12 months
- +1% at 18 months
- +1% at 24 months
- +2% at 30 months

Total voting-enabled by Month 30: 11%

Remaining 9% remains locked.

This structure provides:

- Early-stage blocking capability
- No majority control
- Gradual decentralization

---

# 7. Emission Model

Node Reward Allocation: 500,000,000 BITS

Emission Structure:
- 40-year linear emission
- 12,500,000 BITS per year (1.25%)

Emission begins at DAO activation unless otherwise specified in the Emission Controller contract.

Tokens are voting-enabled upon distribution.

---

# 8. Governance Thresholds

Tier 1 — Operational Decisions: 70% Approval  
Tier 2 — Treasury & Economic Decisions: 70% Approval  
Tier 3 — Constitutional Decisions: 75% Approval  

Approval thresholds apply to participating voting-enabled supply.

---

# 9. Quorum Model

END DAO uses a **Pure Floating Quorum** model.

Quorum = Voting-enabled circulating supply at the time of vote.

No absolute minimum participation floor.

Governance legitimacy derives from participation.

Founders possess early-stage blocking capability due to supermajority structure but do not hold majority control.

---

# 10. Treasury & Operational Funding

Treasury may allocate funds (via governance vote) for:

- Team compensation
- Marketing
- Research & Development
- Infrastructure
- Security audits
- Ecosystem grants

Treasury spending does not alter constitutional token allocation.

Operational compensation is separate from founder allocation.

---

# 11. Buybacks & Capital Deployment

Treasury may, via governance vote:

- Conduct open market buybacks
- Burn repurchased tokens
- Hold tokens in reserve
- Provide liquidity pairing
- Recycle into reward pools

No automatic price support mechanisms exist.

All capital deployment requires governance approval.

---

# 12. Long-Term Design Objective

END DAO is engineered for:

- Multi-decade sustainability
- Gradual diffusion of governance power
- Structural supply discipline
- Transparent stewardship
- Intergenerational continuity

This protocol is designed to mature into decentralization — not simulate it prematurely.
