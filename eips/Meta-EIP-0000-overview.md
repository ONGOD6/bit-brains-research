## 3. Core Definitions (Canonical Vocabulary)

### 3.1 Genesis NFT

A Genesis NFT represents the initial entry asset into the Bit Brains ecosystem.

Genesis assets:

- Are ERC-721 tokens
- May have paired Ethscriptions
- Serve as eligibility anchors for protocol participation
- Enter the Proof-of-Care phase before evolving

Genesis assets do not automatically grant advanced status. Evolution requires measurable continuity.

---

### 3.2 Dual-Asset Architecture

The protocol uses a dual-asset model defined as:

1. ERC-721 NFT (programmable execution asset)
2. Paired Ethscription (immutable provenance artifact)

Each protocol-native ERC-721 may mint a corresponding Ethscription.

Purpose:

- Preserve immutable record layer
- Provide audit trail independent of contract logic
- Anchor upgrades and transformations
- Maintain cross-layer provenance integrity

The dual-asset system does NOT imply two collections.  
It refers specifically to paired rails: programmable + immutable.

---

### 3.3 Proof of Care (PoC)

Proof of Care is the behavioral validation layer of the protocol.

It measures:

- Participation continuity
- Contribution quality
- Protocol-aligned behavior
- Verified identity usage (ENS)
- Community contribution

PoC is not financial staking.  
It is behavioral staking over time.

PoC is measured in epochs.

---

### 3.4 Epoch

An Epoch is a defined measurement period used to:

- Evaluate Proof of Care
- Anchor reward calculations
- Apply emission constraints
- Track continuity scoring

Epochs may be quarterly (4 per year) for emission distribution.

---

### 3.5 CRS (Continuity Reputation Score)

CRS is the scoring engine used to measure:

- Participation consistency
- Task completion quality
- Time continuity
- Alignment with protocol objectives
- Tier modifiers

CRS is not permanent wealth.  
It is a dynamic reputation score.

CRS may influence:

- Node eligibility
- Reward weight
- Governance influence (if enabled)
- Game mechanics

---

### 3.6 Node Games

Node Games are structured behavioral environments designed to:

- Train Genesis participants
- Measure continuity
- Simulate operational states
- Apply CRS modifiers

Node Games are not casual games.  
They are behavioral engines tied to measurable protocol contribution.

---

### 3.7 Node Earn

Node Earn refers to reward acquisition mechanisms tied to:

- Verified participation
- CRS scoring
- Epoch-based emissions

Node Earn does not guarantee returns.  
It reflects weighted distribution based on measurable contribution.

---

### 3.8 Node Burn

Node Burn refers to mechanisms that:

- Reduce token supply
- Reduce reward eligibility
- Apply sacrifice mechanics within games

Node Burn may operate via:
- Transactional mechanics
- Competitive participation
- Governance-approved reductions

---

### 3.9 Node Mode

Node Mode defines operational states of participation.

Examples:

- Training Mode
- Competitive Mode
- Growth Mode
- Governance Mode

Modes may alter CRS weighting or emission logic.

---

### 3.10 Node Grow

Node Grow represents progression scaling based on:

- Sustained Proof of Care
- CRS performance
- Epoch continuity

Growth may unlock:

- Evolution eligibility
- Advanced node roles
- Increased operational responsibility

---

### 3.11 Autonomous Intelligent Technology (AIT)

AIT represents the evolved state of a Genesis participant.

AIT is:

- An upgraded protocol-recognized asset state
- Anchored to measurable continuity
- Not automatically granted at mint

AIT does not imply artificial general intelligence.  
It represents protocol-recognized operational autonomy.

---

### 3.12 Node NFT

A Node NFT represents the evolved asset minted after:

- Sufficient Proof of Care
- Sustained continuity period (e.g., five months)
- Eligibility validation

Node NFTs represent operational participation in the Node Protocol layer.

---

### 3.13 END DAO (Ethereum Node Development DAO)

END DAO governs:

- Emission activation
- Protocol changes
- Treasury allocation
- Long-term development direction

Treasury-held tokens may be excluded from quorum math (as defined in governance EIPs).

---

### 3.14 BITS (Brain Intelligence Technology Services)

BITS is the protocol-native token.

Characteristics:

- Fixed cap (as defined in EIP-0014)
- Long-term emission schedule
- Governance-weighted distribution
- Not guaranteed yield

BITS emissions are epoch-constrained.

## 5. Architectural Principles

The Bit Brains Protocol is designed to:

- Reward continuity over speculation
- Anchor behavior before evolution
- Separate programmable and immutable rails
- Constrain emissions with hard caps
- Enable governance without inflation guarantees

Nothing in this protocol constitutes:

- Guaranteed returns
- Investment contracts
- Profit promises

All participation is voluntary and behaviorally measured.

Genesis → Proof of Care → CRS → AIT → Node

This progression is earned, not granted.
